Dear www-tag,

This is a summary of the TAG's activity from 19 Jan 2003 (end
date of the previous summary [1]) to 13 Feb 2004.

During this period, Mario Jeckle (Daimler-Chrysler) was elected
by the W3C Membership as the newest TAG participant. Welcome,
Mario!

The TAG held two teleconferences and one extended, distributed
video conference during this period. Summaries of those meetings
are linked from the TAG home page [2].

[1] http://lists.w3.org/Archives/Public/www-tag/2004Jan/0072
[2] http://www.w3.org/2001/tag/

1) Architecture Document

  The TAG continues to receive comments about the Last Call
  Architecture Document. The Last Call review period for the
  Architecture Document ends 5 March 2004, at 23:59 ET.
  http://www.w3.org/TR/2003/WD-webarch-20031209/

  For more information about the review:
     http://lists.w3.org/Archives/Public/www-tag/2003Dec/0168
     
2) Issues

  The TAG accepted one issue during this period:

    xmlChunk-44: Chunk of XML - Canonicalization and equality

  The TAG discussed the following issues: rdfmsQnameUriMapping-6,
  whenToUseGet-7, namespaceDocument-8, mixedNamespaceMeaning-13,
  charmodReview-17, qnameAsId-18, contentTypeOverride-24,
  xmlChunk-44

  Details about discussions and related actions are available in
  the meeting minutes linked from the TAG's home page. The TAG's
  issues list is available at: http://www.w3.org/2001/tag/ilist

3) Findings

  The TAG published no new findings during this period.

  Ian Jacobs revised one draft finding:
   "Client Handling of Authoritative Metadata"
   http://www.w3.org/2001/tag/doc/mime-respect-20040127

  The TAG continues to work on a number of other draft
  findings. More information about TAG findings is available at:
    http://www.w3.org/2001/tag/findings

4) Communication

  The TAG invited several Internationalization Working Group
  participants to the 26 January teleconference to discuss the
  TAG's Last Call review of the Character Model specification.

5) Upcoming events

  The TAG will meet face-to-face on 2 March during the Technical
  Plenary Week. The TAG is also planning to hold joint meetings
  with a number of other W3C Working Groups to discuss issues of
  common interest.

  The Technical Plenary Day agenda [3] includes a session with 
  the TAG:
      "Architecture of the World Wide Web and Hot TAG Topics"

  [3] http://www.w3.org/2004/03/TechPlenAgenda.html
  
For co-Chairs Stuart Williams and Tim Berners-Lee,
