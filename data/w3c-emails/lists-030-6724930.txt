September 8, 2003 - The online magazine scifidimensions
( http://www.scifidimensions.com ) is pleased to announce the 
nominees for the 2003 Southeastern Science Fiction Achievement Award 
(the SESFA):

Best Novel of 2002 (Works >40,000 words) ? In alphabetical order
     Chindi by Jack McDevitt (Ace)
     Guardian by Joe Haldeman (Ace)
     Light Music by Kathleen Ann Goonan (EOS)
     Spaceland by Rudy Rucker (Tor)
     Speaks the Nightbird by Robert R. McCammon (River City Pub.)

Best Short Fiction of 2002 (Works <40,000 words) ? In
alphabetical order
     "The Big Rock Candy Mountain" by Andy Duncan 
     "The Cage" by Jeff VanderMeer
     "The Holy Bright Number" by Andy Duncan 
     "Mammoth Dawn" by Gregory Benford

Lifetime Achievement in SF/F/H ? In alphabetical order
     Fred Chappell
     L. Sprague de Camp
     Joe Haldeman
     DeForest Kelley

Participants who pay a modest $7.00 membership fee may vote on the 
Nominees through October 31, 2003.  The winners will be announced no 
later than November 5, 2003.

The SESFA Award, now in its second year, is designed to honor 
accomplishment in science fiction, fantasy or horror by individuals 
born or living in the Southern United States.  The SESFA is 
administered under the auspices of the online science fiction 
magazine scifidimensions.  Details about the SESFA can be found at 
http://www.scifidimensions.com/sesfa/

scifidimensions is an online science fiction magazine edited by John 
C. Snider.  Published monthly since February 2000, scifidimensions 
covers books, movies, television, comics, science and other topics 
of interest to genre fans.

The SESFA Award is sponsored by:
