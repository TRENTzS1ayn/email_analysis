>   We seem to be suggesting that on the one hand prompts require an

>   noticeable encouragement without demanding immediate author response.

fit with Marjolein definitions of alert and prompt.  In other words, if we
changed the term "requires" to "requests", then the definition of "Prompt"
would read:

A prompt requests an author response.
