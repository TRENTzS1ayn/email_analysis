>> THE ASSOCIATION OF ACCESS ENGINEERING SPECIALISTS ANNOUNCES THE WINNERS OF
>> THEIR 1999 EXCELLENCE IN ACCESS AWARDS
>> 
>> Medway, MA, June 25, 1999. The Association of Access Engineering
>> Specialists (AAES) today announced the winners of their 1999 Excellence in
>> Access Awards.  The award winners are:
>> 
>> * The Cellular Telecommunications Industry Association for its training
>> program entitled, "Section 255: Making the Connection" 
>> 
>> * The Worldwide Web Consortium's Web Accessibility Initiative for its "Web
>> Content Accessibility Guidelines" 
>> 
>> * CPB/WGBH National Center for Accessible Media for its Motion Picture
>> Access Technologies
>> 
>> * U.S. Department of Education, Office of the Chief Information Officer,
>> Assistive Technology Team for its Implementation of Requirements for
>> Accessible Software  
>> 
>> * Nokia for its LPS-1 Loopset - Access Innovation Award
>> 
>> * Royal Bank and NCR Corporation for their Audio Banking Machine
>> 
>> * Luxury TowVan Corporation for their Wheelchair Lift Equipped Tow
>> Vehicles - Access Innovation Award 
>> 
>> An awards ceremony, honoring this years winners, will be held during The
>> Rehabilitation Engineering and Assistive Technology Society of North
>> America's (RESNA), "Spotlight on Technology" conference being held in Long
>> Beach, CA on June 25-29, 1999; 
>> http://www.resna.org/resna99/prelim_prog.htmlhttp://www.resna.org/resna99/
>> prelim_prog.html
>> 
>> Many of the winners of AAES' 1999 Excellence in Access Awards will be
>> available, to discuss their accessible design methodologies, at AAES'
>> Disability Access Technical Development Workshop which is being held as a
>> follow-on to the RESNA Conference.  This workshop is conducted on June 30
>> and July 1, 1999 in Long Beach, CA.
>> http://www.narte.org/aaes/aaesnew.html#work
>>

>> About AAES: 
>> 
>> AAES, the Association of Access Engineering Specialists
>> http://www.narte.org/aaes.html was formed as a specialist group under The
>> National Association of Radio and Telecommunications Engineers (NARTE)
>> http://www.narte.org/ and in partnership with, The Rehabilitation
>> Engineering and Assistive Technology Society of North America (RESNA)
>> http://www.resna.org/ in 1997.  
>> AAES Objectives:
>> 
>> * To develop a body of knowledge and resource base for the design and
>> development of accessible products and services.
>> 
>> * To encourage the development of access techniques and promote standards
>> for access compatibility; and to convene industry/consumer forums as
>> needed.
>> 
>> * To provide a vehicle to present problems, recommendations, solutions and
>> new developments.
>> 
>> * To support an ongoing dialog between the disability communities and
>> industry regarding access issues.
