Gregg Vanderheiden forwarded your question about what 'content' should be
taken to mean as it is used in the WAI context to the wai-xtech list.  This
term is certainly worth some more explaining.  The wai-xtech list is a
quiet corner where we discuss miscellaneous cross-group detailed issues,
including particularly the alignment of terminology.

You and/or your implementer friends might want to subscribe to the list
while this is being discussed there.  You can also follow the discussions
in the Web archive for the list.

For the immediate prior time this concept came up (not definitively
resolved, mind you) start with

  wai-xtech@w3.org from May 2001: RE: Glossary: Web Content

  http://lists.w3.org/Archives/Public/wai-xtech/2001May/0026.html

To suscribe send mail to wai-xtech-request@w3.org with the word subscribe
in the subject line.

If you encounter any difficulty with that send mail to the same address
with the word help in the subject line.

If all else fails send mail to that address with a subject line that starts
Re: as if you were replying and it will get to a human and not just be
eaten by the bot.
